04 — About

A developer who actually sweats the details.

I care about the stuff clients feel: pages that load fast, work on every screen, read clearly, and quietly nudge visitors toward doing the thing. I write clean, well-commented code, and I'm just as happy on the front end as I am wiring up an API, a database, or a background job on the back end.

Beyond websites, I build small tools and apps, and I automate the boring bits with n8n and friends — so the repetitive work runs itself while you get on with the actual business.

Single landing page, full business site, SaaS, redesign, custom tool, or a workflow that saves you an hour a day — I'll build it properly and keep you in the loop the whole way.

05 — How I work

Six steps, zero drama.

  1. 01

    Listen

    You tell me the problem and who it's for. I ask the annoying questions now so there are no surprises later.

  2. 02

    Plan

    Scope, stack, and a clear plan. You'll know what you're getting, roughly when, and for how much.

  3. 03

    Design

    A clean, on-brand layout that puts your message and your users first — not my ego.

  4. 04

    Build

    Clean, responsive, well-commented code. Fast pages, real accessibility, no mystery spaghetti.

  5. 05

    Ship

    Deployed and live on Render, Vercel, or wherever fits — with the boring config handled.

  6. 06

    Support

    Tweaks, fixes, and automations after launch. I don't vanish the moment the invoice clears.

06 — FAQ

The questions I usually get.

Are you actually available for work?

Yes — that little green dot at the top isn't decoration. I take on freelance web & software projects, big and small.

What kind of projects do you take on?

Landing pages, multi-page business sites, full-stack web apps, REST APIs, SaaS, redesigns, PWAs, and automations. If it lives on the web (or talks to it), I can probably build it.

Do you really do automation and n8n?

I do. If you've got a repetitive task — moving data between tools, sending alerts, syncing sheets — I can wire it up with n8n (or code) so it just runs itself. I also build small custom apps and tools for one-off needs.

Will my site work on phones?

Every single time. Responsive isn't an add-on, it's the baseline. Same goes for accessibility and fast load times.

How do we start?

Email me a few sentences about what you need. I'll reply quickly with questions, a rough plan, and a price. No pressure, no jargon.
